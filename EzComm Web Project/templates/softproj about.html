    A Communication Interface for the Vocally and Hearing Impaired

WHAT WE DO:


          Gesture recognition is an effort towards studying the challenges in the classification of characters in Indian Sign Language (ISL). A lot of research has already been done in the corresponding field of American Sign Language (ASL), but unfortunately the same cannot be said for ISL. This project deals with the recognition and detection of hand gestures. Images of the hand gestures are taken using a camera and matched with the images in the dataset and the best match is returned. Lack of standard datasets, occluded features and variation in the language with locality have been the major barriers which have led to a little research being done in Indian Sign Language. Our project aims to reduce the communication gap between the vocally disabled people and the normal people who do not know sign language at all. This project detects alphabets (A-Z) and numbers (0-9) in Indian Sign Language. It also converts Speech to Text, Text to Speech, Text to Sign and Speech to Sign.


STEPS:


  • NOTE: This project works only on Indian Sign Language.


  • On opening the model page of EZComm, you will be asked to choose between two options: Sign detection and Sign conversion.


  • In the sign detection page there are three options: Set histogram, numerical sign and alphabet sign. First of all, you will have to click the Set histogram button and configure your hand. Then if you want the program to detect numbers (0-9) then click on the numerical sign button. If you want the program to detect alphabets (A-Z) then click on the alphabet sign button.


  • In the Sign conversion page there are four options: Speech to Text, Text to Speech, Text to Sign and Speech to Sign. You can perform the tasks you want by clicking the respective buttons.
